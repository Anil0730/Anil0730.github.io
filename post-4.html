How can AI save Polar Bears

With all of the environmental changes affecting the Arctic sea ice, polar bears are having a difficult time surviving. There are only 25,000 polar bears in the world and these numbers could drop even further as the ice continues to melt. The San Diego Zoo and Safari Park have teamed up with aerospace contractor Northrop Grumman Corp. to develop an autonomous hexacopter drone to monitor changes in ice and polar bear populations. The technology is strong enough to withstand the harsh environment in the Arctic Circle, allowing a drone to be brought on an expedition to study the behavior of the polar bears as the seasons get longer and the ice is freezing later. The unmanned aerial vehicle will use sensors to map and understand the sea ice formations. It uses a DJI Matrice 600 Pro hexacopter, with a “sensor pod” containing an extremely high-resolution DSLR camera, a five-band multispectral sensor generally marketed for crop monitoring, and a radar to measure ice thickness. The drone will fly over a given path but it will also use a machine learning algorithm that will help the operators know where the polar bears are. The AI will hopefully learn how to track polar bears and help the researchers learn more about how global warming is affecting their population.

In the Arctic, where polar bears and humans share space, there’s a creative push to develop cutting-edge technologies that act as a safeguard for both species. Among these innovations is the AI-powered “Bear-dar” radar system, designed to serve as an early-warning mechanism. This technology utilizes artificial intelligence to detect polar bear movements, providing communities with essential alerts to avoid unexpected encounters. Additionally, satellite-tracking devices, playfully named “Burr on fur,” are being deployed. These devices offer a means to monitor the bears’ movements, helping researchers and communities stay informed about their locations and activities. Moreover, a range of deterrents is being explored to discourage polar bears from approaching human settlements. These include a combination of sounds, strobe lights, scents, and even electrified door mats. The aim is to create an environment where polar bears perceive potential risks, deterring them from venturing too close to inhabited areas. This innovative approach recognizes the need for non-lethal methods to foster coexistence, acknowledging the importance of both human safety and the preservation of polar bear populations. By combining artificial intelligence, satellite tracking, and deterrent systems, researchers and communities are actively striving to create a harmonious and safe environment for both polar bears and humans in the rapidly changing Arctic landscape.

AI is utilized to analyze satellite imagery and identify areas suitable for polar bear habitat preservation in several ways:

1.Machine Learning Algorithms : Researchers use machine learning algorithms to analyze high-resolution satellite imagery and identify patterns in polar bear habitats. These algorithms can detect subtle changes in the environment, such as shifts in sea ice coverage, which are critical for polar bears.

2. Object Detection : AI-powered object detection techniques are used to identify specific features within the satellite imagery, such as areas with suitable sea ice conditions or habitats with abundant food sources. This helps conservationists to pinpoint areas that are crucial for polar bear survival.

3. Image Processing : Advanced image processing techniques are applied to enhance the resolution and clarity of the satellite imagery, allowing for more accurate identification of polar bear habitats. This includes techniques such as image filtering, thresholding, and segmentation.

4. Transfer Learning : Researchers leverage transfer learning to adapt AI models developed for other applications to polar bear habitat analysis. This enables the models to learn from existing data and improve their performance in identifying polar bear habitats.

5. Integration with Local Expert Knowledge : AI models are integrated with local expert knowledge to ensure that the identified habitats are indeed suitable for polar bears. This collaboration between AI and human expertise ensures that the conservation efforts are effective and targeted.

By combining these AI-based techniques with local knowledge, researchers can identify and protect areas that are critical for polar bear survival, ultimately contributing to the conservation of these endangered species.
